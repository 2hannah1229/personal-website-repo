EXPERIENCE

Southern California Edison

I worked on advancing smart meter technology by building a proof of concept for SCE’s first local fault detection application. Using a Real-Time Digital Simulator (RTDS), I modeled and triggered different fault scenarios, then analyzed the resulting data with Python. To better display the data, I created interactive Power BI dashboards that summarized performance trends and insights, helping guide a $53M meter purchase decision decision. Apart from this, I worked with executives across operations and compliance to establish installation standards. I developed presentations, coordinated discussions, and built consensus around cost, benefits, and urgency, which ultimately enabled the rollout of over 1,500 devices across 11 circuits. I also contributed to projects that strengthened SCE’s engineering systems. This included refining bulk power system circuit models for greater accuracy, creating an interface for the SEL-3530 automation controller to move projects from design to testing, creating an RTDS model based on circuit maps, and organizing GIS datasets to support augmented reality tools for improved risk visualization.

Computational Chemistry Intern | California State University, Fullerton

I investigated how secondary organic aerosols form by evaluating thermodynamic parameters (ΔE, ΔH, and ΔG) of reactions between radicals and organic compounds. Using Gaussian 06 on a UNIX system and Excel for calculations, I optimized molecular geometries to improve the accuracy of predictions. Throughout the project, I collaborated with a team of researchers, presented findings at weekly meetings, and contributed to a final report that summarized the results and implications of the study.

Pharmacology Intern | Western University of Health Sciences

I focused on reviewing the literature surrounding bifunctional opioid compounds, with a particular emphasis on agents that engage both NOP and MOP receptors. Through this work, I analyzed the pharmacological profiles of compounds such as BU08028, AT-121, and BU12004, highlighting their potential for pain management with reduced side effects. My work synthesized findings from preclinical studies, including assays like spinal nerve ligation, Von Frey testing, and tail flick experiments, to evaluate efficacy and safety. By comparing mechanisms of action and therapeutic implications, I identified key trends and gaps in current research. This experience strengthened my ability to critically assess scientific data and contextualize novel compounds within the evolving field of opioid pharmacology.

Cancer Research Intern | Biola University

I worked with breast (MCF7, BT-474) and colorectal (HCT116) cancer cell lines, focusing on culturing, passaging, and viability assays. My research tested natural compounds like curcumin and Karella oil delivered through nanomaterials such as MMT clays and liposomes. A major part of my work involved data analysis, where I used Excel to visualize treatment effects on MCF7 cells, quantify viability, and interpret release profiles from assays like crystal violet and UV-vis spectroscopy. I presented these findings at a research symposium, contributing to program visibility and scientific discussion. This experience strengthened my skills in experimental design, data visualization, and science communication.
